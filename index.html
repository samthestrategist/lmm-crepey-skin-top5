Why Most Anti-Aging Skin Supplements Don't Work

Here's what happens inside your skin after age 40:

The Collagen Collapse Cascade Explained:

After age 40, your body loses 1-2% of its collagen EVERY YEAR. But that's just the first domino.

Collagen is a triple-helix protein that provides structure and firmness. When collagen production drops, your skin loses its "scaffolding." But collagen doesn't work alone. It depends on:

Elastin: The protein that makes your skin bounce back when you stretch it. After 40, elastin fibers break down faster than they're replaced. Result: sagging, especially around the jawline, neck, and décolletage.

Hyaluronic Acid: The molecule that holds 1,000 times its weight in water. It keeps skin plump and hydrated. After menopause, hyaluronic acid production drops by 50%. Result: volume loss, hollowing under eyes, thinning lips.

Collagen Cross-Linking: Requires vitamin C and copper to stabilize. Without adequate levels, new collagen is weak and degrades quickly.

This isn't one ingredient deficiency. It's a cascading collapse of the entire dermal matrix.

A 2014 study in Skin Pharmacology and Physiology tested marine collagen peptides (2.5g/day) in 114 women over 8 weeks. Skin elasticity improved by 15%. But here's the key finding: women who ALSO supplemented with vitamin C, hyaluronic acid, and antioxidants (astaxanthin, CoQ10) saw 28% improvement — nearly double.

That's the problem with most collagen supplements: they address one piece of the cascade and ignore the others.
